Neuroscience

How Neuroplasticity Works

Your brain physically changes with every deliberate practice session. Neurons that fire together, wire together.

🧠

Synaptic Strengthening

Repeated activation strengthens neural connections through long-term potentiation (LTP), making signals faster and stronger.

⚡

Myelination

Practice speeds up signal transmission by wrapping a myelin sheath around frequently-used neural pathways.

🌱

Neurogenesis

Novel cognitive challenges stimulate the growth of new neurons in the hippocampus, the brain's memory hub.

🔄

Cross-Hemispheric Transfer

Bimanual and dual-task exercises strengthen the corpus callosum, improving whole-brain coordination.

Philosophy

Deliberate Practice, Not Gamification

This is not a game. There are no points to chase. Each exercise here is a tool for genuine cognitive adaptation — designed around the neuroscience of how brains actually change.

"It is not a metaphor to say that the brain rewires itself. Neurons that fire together, wire together — and the brain that performs one act over and over for many hours is physically different from the brain before practice."

— Norman Doidge, The Brain That Changes Itself

The exercises here are drawn from cognitive neuroscience, sports psychology, and rehabilitative neurotherapy. They range from visual processing drills used by elite athletes, to working memory protocols from clinical research, to bilateral coordination techniques pioneered in stroke rehabilitation.
